To be confident that Wait Wise “does not collect data beyond what is declared,” you need to do two things:

1) Verify what the app actually collects/transmits (runtime reality)  
2) Make the App Privacy answers match that reality (App Store Connect disclosure)

Here’s a practical workflow that usually clears this checklist item and prevents privacy-related rejections.

---

## 1) Inventory what “data collection” means (Apple’s definition)
Apple considers data “collected” if your app (or SDKs in it) transmits data off-device that can relate to a user/device, including for analytics, advertising, account features, etc. (This maps to App Store Connect’s App Privacy section.)

Common hidden sources:
- Analytics SDKs (Firebase, Amplitude, Mixpanel, Sentry, etc.)
- Ad SDKs
- Crash reporting
- “Sign in with …” providers
- Any backend API that receives identifiers, logs, or content
- Embedded web content that loads third-party scripts/trackers

---

## 2) Check what you *actually* send (the most reliable method)
### A. Inspect your network traffic
Do one of these:
- Xcode: Run on device → use debugging tools to observe network calls (and logs).  
- Proxy tool: Proxyman / Charles Proxy to capture requests from the app and see:
  - What endpoints are called
  - What payload fields are sent (email, device ID, location, etc.)
  - Whether third-party domains are contacted (analytics/ads)

Make a simple table like:
- Destination (domain)
- Purpose (auth, analytics, crash, content)
- Data fields sent

### B. Check iOS permissions used
Look at what your app requests:
- Location, Contacts, Photos, Microphone, Camera, Bluetooth, etc.  
If you request them, confirm whether that data is actually transmitted off-device and why.

### C. Audit third-party SDKs
List all SDKs and what they collect by default. Many collect device identifiers, IP, usage events unless configured off.

If you tell me what libraries you’re using (Firebase? Sentry? ads?), I can tell you the common “default collection” traps and what to switch off.

---

## 3) Make App Store Connect “App Privacy” match reality
In App Store Connect → App Privacy, for each data type, you must specify:
- Collected or not
- If collected: whether it’s linked to the user or used for tracking
- The purpose (Analytics, App Functionality, etc.)

Important:
- If you use anything that qualifies as Tracking, you also need ATT prompt and to declare tracking usage correctly.
- If you do not collect anything, ensure you truly have no third-party analytics/crash reporting sending device/user data.

Apple rejects apps under guideline 5.1.1 (Data Collection and Storage) when disclosures don’t match observed behavior.

---

## 4) Align the Privacy Policy + in-app messaging
If you declare collection, your Privacy Policy should explicitly describe it (what, why, retention, sharing).  
If you declare “no data collected,” ensure your policy doesn’t contradict that (some templates accidentally say you collect analytics/identifiers).

---

## 5) Final “awareness” checklist you can run before submission
- [ ] No unexpected third-party domains in network logs  
- [ ] SDKs audited and configured (or removed)  
- [ ] App Privacy section matches actual data flows  
- [ ] Privacy Policy matches App Privacy answers  
- [ ] ATT used if tracking applies (otherwise tracking = No)
